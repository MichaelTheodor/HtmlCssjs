Extra baggage & Overweight baggage

Extra baggage is any bag exceeding the free checked baggage allowance corresponding to your fare. For example, the first piece of baggage if you have a Light or Flex ticket, the second piece of baggage if you have a ComfortFlex ticket or the third piece of baggage if you are travelling Business Class.

Overweight baggage is considered any checked baggage weighing over 23kg and up to 32kg. Baggage weighing over 32kg can only be accepted as cargo.

The charges listed in the below table apply in cases of extra and overweight baggage. If you wish to carry an extra piece of baggage which also happens to be overweight, you will incur an additional charge.

Business Class

Special assistance to physically challenged passengers

GREEK AIRLINES provides all the necessary services to ensure that passengers with special needs can enjoy a comfortable flight and board/disembark the aircraft with absolute safety.

Passengers with special needs can transport 2 items of mobile equipment at no extra cost. Please keep in mind that mobile equipment used for sports, can also be transported free of charge, as long as it is included within the above two item allowance (two pieces in total).

If you require assistance at the airport or during your boarding/disembarkation you can either, request special assistance, online during the booking process or via My booking. In any case, please make sure your request is submitted at least 48 hours prior to your departure.

Passengers with certain medical conditions, those requiring special care during the flight, as well as passengers in the post-operative period may be required to provide a Medical Information Form (MEDIF). The MEDIF is completed by the passenger's attending physician, giving details on the patient's state of health and certifying that he or she is fit to fly.

For more information about assistance at the airport or during boarding/disembarkation, please contact our reservations department, which is available 24 hours a day at 8018010101(calls from local landline) or at +30 2103550500 (calls from mobiles and international calls).

In order to simplify the boarding process, please be advised that you can check-in online 48 hours prior to your flight and print your boarding pass at the airport. Athens International Airport "Eleftherios Venizelos" and Thessaloniki Airport "Makedonia" offer special check-in counters exclusively for people with special needs. Please note that you must arrive at the airport at least 60 minutes prior to your flight.

For any questions or complaints, please contact us and through our Call Center at (+30) 210 6261000.

Pregnancy

If you are an expectant mother with a complication-free pregnancy you can travel normally on GREEK AIRLINES flights until the end of the 36th week of pregnancy. After the 36th week of pregnancy you will not be able to board any flight.

Carriage with stretcher

Carriage of a passenger on a stretcher is only provided on domestic flights and international flights operated by Airbus aircraft. If you require this service, you must inform us at least 48 hours prior to your flight. .

Flights to the US

If you are travelling to the United States of America, you must ensure prior to your departure that you comply with the enforced regulations of the country of destination. For further and more accurate information, we advise you to refer to the relevant consulates and embassies.
GREEK AIRLINES will not be authorized to board passengers who have not carried out the mandatory formalities.

VISA WAIVER PROGRAM

This program permits citizens of specific countries to enter the USA without a visa for a maximum of 90 days under specific prerequisites, such as an approved ESTA authorization, a return/onward ticket to exit the US, etc. Click on this link to see which countries are eligible: www.dhs.gov

Passport requirements

Passport must be an “e-passport“ (also known as “biometric”). This contains a chip with your biographical, biometric data and a digital passport photograph.

Visa

You will need a visa if your passport does not meet these requirements or you are not a citizen of a country participating in the Visa Waiver Program.

ELECTRONIC SYSTEM FOR TRAVEL AUTHORISATION (ESTA)

Anyone wishing to enter the USA on the Visa Waiver Programme must apply for Electronic Travel Authorisation. Please apply as early as possible. We suggest at least 72 hours prior to your departure. AEGEAN and Olympic Air have the right to refuse transportation for passengers without any proof of valid ESTA travel permission at the time of check-in and/or boarding time. At present, the green entry form (I-94W) must still be completed on board.

APPLY FOR YOUR ESTA TRAVEL AUTHORISATION

Important Notice: As of 8 September 2010, a 14 USD fee will be charged (by payment card) for each ESTA authorisation request.

ESTA Validity

Your ESTA authorisation is valid for up to two years provided it is not withdrawn and your passport does not expire. The ESTA travel authorisation is valid for multiple entries into the United States. You can easily update the destination address, travel route or your future travel plans on the ESTA website.

Important Notice: ESTA request is processed directly on line. You must ensure that the passport number along with the country of issuance details have been correctly entered. Data entered are not automatically checked at the time of the request. In the event of mistake, for instance 10 digits entered instead of 9 for a European passport, you will have no other option than put in another request.

ADVANCED PASSENGER INFORMATION SYSTEM (APIS)

APIS is a requirement under US immigration law. Airlines are obliged to transmit their passenger data to the US Department of Homeland Security prior to departing for the USA. To avoid inconvenience when you arrive in the USA, it is important to make sure that you complete the APIS form correctly.

APIS collects data including surnames, first names, passport numbers, temporary address* where the passenger will be staying, etc.

*The temporary address must include the house number, street, city/town, abbreviation used for the state, and the zip code.

Currently, you can provide APIS data at our check in points at the airport.

Since November the 4th, 2010, passengers travelling to USA are able to complete the APIS required data through the e-ticket service during booking.
